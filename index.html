History in a nutshell

1956 - Artificial Intelligence (AI)

AI is founded as a research field. AI is the ability of a computer or a robot controlled by a computer to do tasks that are usually done by humans because they require human intelligence and discernment.

1980 - XCON

XCON becomes the first industrial expert system to win success. XCON is a Rule-based system written in OPS5 in 1978 to assist in the ordering of DEC's VAX computer systems by automatically selecting the computer system components based on the customer's requirements.

2002 - The Four Colors Theorem is proved using the Coq proof assistance.

The 150 year old Four Colour Theorem (every map drawn on the plane be coloured with four colours so that no two regions having a common border have the same colour), is the first famous result with a proof that required the Coq proof assistant, to write an extended program that specifies both the calculations and their mathematical justification.

2019 - Normative reasoning

A paper by Tomer Libal (our CEO) and Matteo (TBD) about Normative Reasoning is presented in the International Conference on Artifical Intelligence and Law.

2020 - NAI editor

Tomer Libal's and Alex (TBD)'s NAI editor is created, and wins first prize at the IRIS Conference.

2021 - Funding

The legAi project wins the Luxembourgish FNR PoC grant.

2022 - the LegAi Solution

The Ai-dvisor, that combines Artificial Intelligence and Law, is presented.
